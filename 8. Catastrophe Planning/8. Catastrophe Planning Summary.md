# Catastrophe Planning Summary
## Traxis MFG

---

### Purpose

Strategic risk planning to address significant business vulnerabilities and ensure continuity for a sub-10 employee machining company using ProShop for process documentation.

---

### Key Risk Areas Identified

**1. Key Personnel Loss**
- Critical knowledge concentrated in limited staff
- Process documentation via ProShop helps mitigate but doesn't eliminate risk

**2. Economic Downturns**
- Cyclical exposure inherent to machining/manufacturing sector
- Cash reserves and operational flexibility as buffers

**3. Major Customer Dependency (Critical)**
- Single 15-year customer relationship represents ~50% of total revenue
- Growing risk factors:
  - Customer establishing manufacturing facilities elsewhere to avoid tariffs
  - Potential for customer to replicate capabilities domestically
- This risk has become the most urgent priority

---

### Current Focus: Customer Diversification

The broader catastrophe planning discussion has been temporarily tabled to address the immediate need for revenue diversification.

**Active Initiatives:**
- Identifying and researching local target companies
- Drafting outreach communications
- Planning a separate, dedicated Customer Outreach project for systematic business development

**Potential Targets Identified:**
- Saronic (marine drones) — local company

---

### Recognized Challenges

- Historical difficulty with sales, customer outreach, and self-promotion
- Preference for direct, straightforward communication over polished marketing language
- Discomfort with self-promotion that extends beyond sales activities
- Acknowledgment that these underlying patterns need to be addressed

**Support Options Under Consideration:**
- AI-assisted outreach and drafting
- Human professional support (business development, counseling)

---

### Next Steps

1. Establish dedicated Customer Outreach project
2. Develop systematic approach to business development
3. Continue identifying and qualifying potential customers
4. Address underlying visibility/self-promotion challenges
5. Return to broader catastrophe planning once diversification efforts are underway
